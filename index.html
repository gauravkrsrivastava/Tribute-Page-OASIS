Timeline of Bhagat Singh

The events in Bhagat Singh extraordinary life.

Early life
  • September 27, 1907: Bhagat Singh was born on September 27, 1907 in Lyallpur district of British India (now in Pakistan) to Kishan Singh and Vidyavati.
  • 1923: In 1923, Bhagat Singh joined the National College in Lahore, established by Lala Lajpat Rai; a freedom fighter, and a prominent political leader in the Indian Independence movement.
  • Summer 1924: In 1924, he worked with Ganesh Shankar Vidyarthi for Pratap, a revolutionary weekly. In this role, he penned down revolutionary articles and distributed pamphlets outlining principles of a violent uprising, aimed at overthrowing the Britishers.
  • 1926: He founded the Naujawan Bharat Sabha, a socialist organization with the aim to overthrow British rule in India. In 1927, he was arrested and was charged with involvement in Lahore bombing case that took place in 1926. He was released after 5 weeks..
  • 1928: He formed the Hindustan Republican Association (HRA), which later became Hindustan Socialist Republican Army (HSRA). Revolutionaries like Ram Prasad Bismil, Ashfaqullah Khan and Chandrashekhar Azad were also part of it.
  • 1929: In 1929 he and an associate lobbed a bomb at the Central Legislative Assembly in Delhi to protest the implementation of the Defence of India Act and then surrendered. He was hanged at the age of 23 for the murder of Saunders..
Bhagat Singh’s slogans.
  • 1: “They may kill me, but they cannot kill my ideas. They can crush my body, but they will not be able to crush my spirit.”
  • 2: “Labor is the real sustainer of society.”
  • 3: “Revolution is an inalienable right of mankind. Freedom is an imperishable birthright of all.”.
  • 4: “Bombs and pistols do not make a revolution. The sword of revolution is sharpened on the whetting stone of ideas.”
  • 5: “Any man who stands for progress has to criticise, disbelieve and challenge every item of the old faith.”
  • 6: “The aim of life is no more to control the mind, but to develop it harmoniously; not to achieve salvation hereafter, but to make the best use of it here below.”
  • 7: “Merciless criticism and independent thinking are the two necessary traits of revolutionary thinking.”
  • 8: “But man’s duty is to try and endeavour, success depends upon chance and environment.”.
